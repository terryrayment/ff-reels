# About — `/site/about`

## Goal

Answer the three questions someone arriving on this page actually has:

1. Who runs this place?
2. Where are they?
3. How do they work?

…in roughly that order, in as little copy as possible.

## Direction

Canada's "about" is essentially nonexistent — they let the work speak. FF is a smaller, more relationship-driven shop, so a stripped-back about page is worth keeping. But strip the corporate-website padding.

## Proposed structure

1. **Statement** — single sentence, large, no eyebrow. The whole positioning in one line.
2. **Who** — `TEAM` label, then a list of partners with role + email. Direct mailto links preserved.
3. **Where** — `OFFICES` label, two columns (LA, NYC) with addresses.
4. **Network** — `SALES REPS` label, three regions with rep names.
5. **(Optional) Selected clients** — `CLIENTS` label, a comma-separated list of brand names. Logos optional.

## Open questions

- Real addresses for LA and NYC. Placeholders in code right now (`1234 Sunset`, `456 Greene`).
- Founding year? Currently uses "over 25 years of production experience" copy from the existing FF site — that refers to the partners' combined experience, not the company's age. Worth being clearer.
- Do we want a `HISTORY` block? Probably not. If anyone cares, they ask in an email.
- Headshots on the team list — Canada wouldn't. Park Pictures wouldn't. Skip.
