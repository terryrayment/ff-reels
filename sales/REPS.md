## Economics — industry norms

- **Commission:** 5–15%, 10% midpoint
- **Retainer:** $1,500–$6,000/mo typical for boutique
- **Marketing reimbursement:** production company covers; rep doesn't pay for events they throw on your behalf
- **Treatment costs:** $3,000–$15,000 per meaningful bid, borne by production company

## What reps do well (when they do)

- Bid-list intelligence (who's bidding what, when, what budget)
- Boards triage + creative-fit matching
- Pitch advocacy with creative teams
- Relationship hygiene (lunches, screenings, holidays)
- Treatment support / co-management
- PR, reel curation, launches, festival entries
- Career development for directors (the deepest value)

## What reps don't do (despite invoicing)

- Replace your reel, treatment quality, or founder relationships
- Pursue accounts where they don't already have warm contacts
- Break new categories outside their personal background
- Make a passive rep worth 10% + retainer (they become inbox forwards)

| Post-job relationship | Co-maintain | **Lead on top accounts** |
| Crisis on-set/pre-pro | Notified | **Lead** |

**The rule:** the rep owns the funnel, the founder owns the top of the funnel.

## What NOT to do as the founder

- Go around the rep to their agency contacts unannounced
- Undercut reps in front of agency producers
- Over-promise to win a bid
- Bid every job that comes in (a graceful "we pass — wrong fit" earns respect)
- Sell craft you can't deliver
